Glossary
========

This page defines common abbreviations, terms, and acronyms used across the Queen's Space Engineering Team (QSET) documentation.

.. glossary::
   :sorted:

   ADCS
      Attitude Determination and Control System. The subsystem responsible for estimating and controlling the spacecraft's physical orientation (attitude) using sensors (e.g., sun sensors, magnetometers) and actuators (e.g., magnetorquers, reaction wheels).

   OBC
      On-Board Computer. The central computational unit of the satellite. It runs the flight software (FSW), processes telemetry, schedules tasks, handles commands from the ground station, and manages data storage.

   EPS
      Electrical Power System. The subsystem responsible for harvesting solar energy via solar panels, storing energy in lithium-ion battery packs, and distributing regulated power (typically 3.3V, 5V, and 12V rails) to other subsystems.

   Comms
      Communications subsystem. Handles the radio frequency (RF) link between the satellite and the ground station, including modulation, packet encapsulation (e.g., AX.25), transceiver control, and antenna deployment.

   Ground Segment
      The ground station hardware and mission control software used by operators on Earth to track the satellite, transmit telecommands, and receive telemetry data.

   Payload
      The scientific instrument, camera, or experiment that serves as the primary mission objective of the satellite.

   Space School
      QSET's educational outreach and curriculum initiative, focused on teaching orbital mechanics and satellite engineering concepts to high school and elementary students.

   Mech
      Mechanical and structures subsystem. Covers the physical satellite chassis (e.g., Al 6061-T6 structure), thermal control (active/passive), CAD modeling, and environmental vibration testing.

   LEO
      Low Earth Orbit. An orbit around Earth with an altitude between 160 kilometers (99 mi) and 2,000 kilometers (1,200 mi). Most CubeSats operate in LEO.

   PDR
      Preliminary Design Review. A critical systems engineering milestone where the subsystem designs are evaluated to ensure they meet requirements before detailed design begins.

   CDR
      Critical Design Review. A multi-disciplined technical review to ensure that the system can proceed into fabrication, demonstration, and test, and can meet performance requirements.

   TVAC
      Thermal Vacuum. Environmental testing where a component or the complete satellite is placed in a chamber under vacuum conditions and cycled through extreme temperatures to simulate space environment.

   SSOT
      Single Source of Truth. The engineering practice of keeping all design specifications, requirements, and parameters in a single, authoritative repository rather than scattered across chats, drives, and spreadsheets.

   V-Model
      A systems engineering framework that relates development phases (requirements, design) directly to verification phases (testing, integration) to ensure complete coverage and traceability.

   Sphinx-Needs
      A Sphinx extension used in this repository to define, link, and trace engineering requirements, specifications, and test cases directly in text.

   Mermaid
      A markdown-like syntax for generating flowcharts, sequence diagrams, and architecture layouts dynamically from plain text.
